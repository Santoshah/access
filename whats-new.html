Why test for metabolites?

  • Indicates patient is actively taking the medication/drug.
  • Drug metabolism rates vary among patients. Some patients metabolize a drug so rapidly that therapeutically effective blood and tissue concentrations are not reached; in others, metabolism may be so slow that usual doses have toxic effects. Individual drug metabolism rates are influenced by genetic factors, coexisting disorders (particularly chronic liver disorders and advanced heart failure).
  • Can identify substitutions. For example, taking a ‘cheaper’ opioid for therapy, and diverting the more expensive one.
  • Some compounds are extensively metabolized and parent compound can not be detected.
  • Can identify potential drug-drug interactions. Some drugs can increase/decrease metabolism of other drugs.
  • Some parent drugs are excreted quickly while their metabolites are fat soluble and are excreted slowly (THC-OH vs. THC-COOH).
